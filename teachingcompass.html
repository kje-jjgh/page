What is the Teaching Compass?

The OECD Teaching Compass offers a future-focused and holistic vision of what it means to teach and be a teacher. It's not a rigid set of rules, but rather a set of guiding principles to help educators navigate the complexities of modern education.

Inspired by the OECD Learning Compass 2030 for students, this framework empowers teachers to champion student agency, well-being, and the competencies needed to shape a better future for society and the planet.

The 5 Key Concepts of the Teaching Compass

This is your inner anchor. It's about grounding your teaching practice in a strong professional identity and clear ethical values. This anchor helps you navigate complexity, support student learning, and maintain your own well-being. It is built on three pillars: Being (authentic self-awareness), Belonging (mutually supportive relationships), and Becoming (continuous growth).

This concept empowers you with the autonomy, confidence, and skills to initiate and sustain meaningful innovation. Teacher agency means you are not just a deliverer of curriculum, but a co-designer of learning, an adaptive expert, and an agent of change, both individually and collectively with your peers.

Beyond subject knowledge, this focuses on cultivating the critical competencies and adaptive skills needed to manage complexity, uncertainty, and continuous change. This includes a holistic blend of Knowledge (of self, learner, world), Skills (cognitive, socio-emotional, practical), and Attitudes & Values (growth mindset, empathy, integrity).

You cannot pour from an empty cup. This concept promotes comprehensive well-being strategies to ensure teachers flourish personally and professionally. It recognizes that teacher well-being (physical, cognitive, socio-emotional) is not a luxury, but essential for sustaining your capacity to educate, inspire, and engage students.

Teaching doesn't happen in a vacuum. This concept positions educators within a collaborative network of students, parents, school leaders, community partners, and policymakers. True transformation requires a multi-stakeholder effort that fosters collective responsibility and systemic improvement.

The Rise of AI: Human Teachers and AI Agents

The Teaching Compass acknowledges the growing role of AI. The goal isn't replacement, but **optimal integration**. The table below highlights the unique strengths of human intelligence and how they can be complemented by AI.

Aspect Human Intelligence (The Teacher) Artificial Intelligence (The Tool)
Empathy & Relational Skills Grounded in emotional awareness and lived experience. Builds trust and responds to non-verbal cues. Inspires and mentors. Simulates empathetic responses based on data. Lacks genuine feeling or understanding. Can provide structured, non-judgmental feedback.
Creativity & Innovation Generates original ideas and makes intuitive leaps based on emotion, context, and experience. Detects patterns and generates content based on existing data. Lacks authentic creative intent.
Ethical Reasoning & Judgment Possesses a moral conscience. Can weigh complex, value-driven decisions and is accountable for them. Operates within pre-programmed parameters. Lacks moral agency or accountability. Can enforce rules consistently.
Adaptability Dynamically adapts to unexpected social and emotional situations in the classroom. Adapts based on programmed learning. Can rapidly personalize content delivery based on student performance data.
Optimal Integration Teachers focus on the human elements: empathy, judgment, and relationship-building. AI handles data-driven tasks: personalizing learning paths, automating grading, and providing administrative support, freeing up teachers for high-impact interaction.

Reflection for Your Practice

The Teaching Compass is a tool for reflection and growth. As you move forward, consider these questions:

  • Anchor: What are the core values that anchor my teaching? How can I strengthen my sense of belonging in my school community?
  • Agency: Where in my practice can I exercise more agency to innovate for my students?
  • Competencies: What is one new skill or area of knowledge I want to develop this year?
  • Well-being: What is one small, concrete step I can take this week to support my own well-being?
